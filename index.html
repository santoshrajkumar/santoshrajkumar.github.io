Hello and welcome!

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at The Ohio State University and a Graduate Research Associate in the System Optimization and Autonomous Robotics (SOAR) Lab, advised by Dr. Debdipta Goswami.

I am broadly interested in learning-based estimation and control, operator-theoretic methods for modeling and controlling nonlinear systems, optimal control, and the innovative integration of AI and machine learning into systems and control. A key focus of my work is developing techniques for learning in dynamic systems that operate under noisy and constrained measurement conditions. I am particularly interested in applying these approaches to tackle complex challenges in aerospace systems, robotics, and other autonomous systems.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I pursued my MS at Miami University, where I designed and experimentally validated haptic rendering techniques for large touch interfaces using . My undergraduate research at the National Institute of Technology, Silchar, focused on wireless networked control systems.

Beyond academia, I bring more than three years of industry experience at Indian Oil Corporation Limited (94th in Fortune 500 list 2022), where I worked on automation, vibration monitoring, and robotic inspections.

If you share my interest in advancing autonomous systems through innovative control strategies and operator-theoretic methods, I would be delighted to connect.
