Everything on F1 Visa Application

Published 3 months ago5 min read4 comments

In the United States, the F visas are a type of non-immigrant student visa that allows foreigners to pursue education (academic studies and/or language training programs) in the United States. F-1 students must maintain a full course of study. F-1 visas are only issued in U.S. embassies and consulates outside the United States, although extensions of stay and changes of status may be possible within the United States. Prospective F-1 students must apply at the schools and receive a form I-20 in order to apply for an F-1 visa. F-1 students must show that they are able to support themselves during their stay in the U.S., as their opportunities for legal employment are quite limited.

The First Step : Admit & I-20

The first step for F1 visa application is to receive offer of admission from a US university / institute. After you receive admit from a university you wish to attend, you need to apply for I-20 from that university. The I-20 is a comprehensive document containing your admit information, annual cost of attendance, funds info, and SEVIS ID. Be aware that almost all universities ask for proof of financial support (may be graduate assistantship, scholarship, personal funds, parents' funds or any other person's funds supporting you) while issuing an I-20.

SEVIS Fee Payment

After receiving you I-20, you may pay the SEVIS (Student and Exchange Visitor Information System) fee ($350) with the SEVIS id mentioned in your I-20 here -> SEVIS / I-901 portal After payment, the system will generate a receipt and keep it safely. You must pay the SEVIS fee at least one day prior to your Visa interview.

Non-immigrant Visa Application

Your official US non-immigrant Visa application is known as DS-160. Start it here -> DS-160. DS-160 is a long form to be filled online, fill it carefully and don't lie there. After you submit your DS-160 application a conformation page will come up. Get it printed and keep it safe.

Hack: If you are not sure where (eg. Delhi, Kolkata, Mumbai etc.) to submit the application, then you may just start your application and get the confirmation number. This number can be used to book Visa appointment even though the application is incomplete. After booking an appointment, you can complete and submit the DS-160 application to the respective city where you got Visa interview appointment.

Schedule Visa Appointments

It's time to get Visa appointments. Create an account & login here -> cgifederal.secure.force.com. Once you login and start the process by entering your details, you'll be promted to pay the Visa MRV fee ($165). There are options to pay like NEFT, IMPS etc. NEFT from netbanking works fine most of the time. After you make the paymennt, it may take several hours to get reflected in your account. Once that is reflected, you will be able to look for appointment dates.

There are two stages of Visa appointements:
  • OFC / VAC Appointement (Where Biometrics, Photograph are taken and DS-160 confirmation page is stamped)
  • Consular Appointment (Where a Consular Officer takes interview to approve your Visa)

Even if OFC dates are available, your appointment will not be confirmed unless both the types are available. Keep looking for it. Also, keep in mind that there must be at least 1 day gap between your OFC date and Consular appointment date. You may choose to either pick up option or premium delivery option for delivering your stamped passport.

Don't log in more than thrice to check available dates. Otherwise you'll be blocked for 72 hours. Ideally try twice in different time of the day. The best way is to join Telegram, Whatsapp groups where updates on availability of appointments are available.
